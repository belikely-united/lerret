# Licensing

Lerret is **dual-licensed**: a free open-source license for the community, and a paid commercial license for companies that need different terms.

## 1. Open source — AGPL-3.0 (free)

Lerret is licensed under the [GNU Affero General Public License v3.0](LICENSE). You may use, modify, self-host, and redistribute it **for free**, including inside a company.

The one condition that matters: **if you run a _modified_ version of Lerret as a network service** (a hosted / SaaS offering), the AGPL requires you to make the complete source of your modified version available to that service's users under the AGPL.

For almost everyone — individuals, teams self-hosting, anyone running the CLI or studio locally — the AGPL costs nothing and asks nothing.

## 2. Commercial license (paid)

If the AGPL doesn't fit, a commercial license is available. You likely need one if you want to:

- offer Lerret (modified or not) to others **as a hosted or commercial service** without releasing your source under the AGPL;
- **embed or bundle** Lerret inside a closed-source / proprietary product;
- otherwise use Lerret free of the AGPL's copyleft obligations.

The commercial license lifts the AGPL requirements and can be tailored to your use.

**Contact:** `oss@belikely.com`

## Why dual licensing?

Lerret is built openly and stays genuinely free for the community under the AGPL. The commercial license exists so that well-resourced companies who want to build a business *on top of* Lerret contribute back — either in source (via the AGPL) or commercially. That's what funds continued open development. This is the same model used by Grafana, Plausible, Cal.com, and many other open-source projects.
